🎨 Task 1 — Photoshop: Content-Aware Fill & Artist Research (60 min)
Document setup: File → New → A3 Landscape · 420×297mm · 300 dpi · CMYK

Step-by-Step — click each step to expand

1
Prepare Your Document
▼
  • File → New → A3 Landscape | 300 dpi | CMYK
  • Add white background: Edit → Fill → White
2
Import Your Physical Work
▼
  • Drag and drop your scan into Photoshop, or use File → Place Embedded…
  • Move Tool (V) to position · Cmd/Ctrl+T to resize or rotate
3
Content-Aware Fill
▼
  • Use Rectangular Marquee Tool (M) to select an interesting area
  • Cmd/Ctrl+C then Cmd/Ctrl+V — creates a new layer
  • Hide the original layer (👁 eye icon) so the cut-out sits on white
  • Magic Wand Tool (W) → click the white background area
  • Edit → Content-Aware Fill… → preview → OK
Why? Generates new textures and abstract forms from your primary research.
4
Refine & Blend
▼
  • Experiment with blending modes and layer opacity
  • Merge layers when satisfied with the result
5
Add Text & Artist Research
▼
  • Type Tool (T) — add artist name, short quote, brief reflection
  • Recommended fonts: Andale Mono · Orator Std · Typewriter Condensed
  • Print size: 9–14pt · avoid placing text over busy image areas
Artists to explore:
  • Gerhard Richter · Lorna Simpson · Andreas Gursky · Ruth van Beek · Sarah Charlesworth
6
Save Your Work
▼
  • Save as .PSD — keeps layers editable
  • Export as .JPG — for Padlet upload

Every Research Page Needs

🖼️

Primary Research

Your own photos, sketches or observations

🔗

Research Links

Secondary images showing where design ideas came from

✍️

Annotations

Short notes — what you notice, what connects to your design

🎨

Artist Reference

At least one named artist with a brief connection explained

📚

Sources Credited

All secondary images referenced — see Task 3

🗂️

Clear Title

A heading or subheading that describes the page

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Images with no annotations — thinking must be visible
  • No research links — show where ideas came from
  • Sources uncredited — all images must be acknowledged
  • Text that dominates — keep typography proportionate

📚 Task 3 — Harvard Referencing (20 min)
Why reference? It credits artists and designers, shows genuine research, and is a required assessed element of your portfolio.

The Two Parts

1. In-Text Citation

Added on your page next to the image or quote.

Format

(Surname, Year)

Example

(Richter, 1988)

2. Reference List

Full details at the end of your portfolio section.

Artwork

Artist, I. (Year) Title [Medium]. City: Gallery.

Website

Author (Year) Title. Available at: URL (Accessed: Date).

💡 Quick Rules

  • Artworks: always include medium in [square brackets]
  • Websites: always include the date accessed
  • Reference list: alphabetical order by surname
  • 3+ authors: use et al. in citations — list all in reference list

  • Use evaluative language — "this worked because…", "this was difficult because…"
  • Aim for 3–5 sentences per question
